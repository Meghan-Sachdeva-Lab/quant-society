Core Mathematical Models We Use

Quantitative finance relies on converting price data into concrete formulas. Here are two baseline frameworks we cover in our labs:

1. Simple Moving Average (SMA)

To smooth out daily erratic price movements and isolate general trends, we compute an arithmetic mean over a specific lookback window (n days):

SMA = (P₁ + P₂ + ... + Pₙ) / n

*Where P represents the closing price on a given day, and n is the total number of days.

2. Simple Percentage Return

Instead of tracking nominal dollar changes, models track percentage returns to standardize volatility metrics across varying stock price scales:

Return (%) = [(P_current - P_initial) / P_initial] × 100
